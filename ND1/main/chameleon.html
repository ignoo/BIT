Jackson's chameleon
Furcifer pardalis Chamaeleo calyptratus Trioceros jacksonii
Originates from eastern and northern Madagascar Originates from Saudi Arabia and Yemen Originates from East Africa
They are one of the most colorful of all chameleons, particularly the male chameleon. They are known for their variable color changes due to a variety of factors, including to show aggression, social status, reproduction, and stress. They are sometimes called three-horned chameleons because males possess three brown horns
 

Parson's chameleon

Brookesia nana
Calumma parsonii Brookesia nana
Originates from eastern and northern Madagascar Originates from northern Madagascar
The Parson's chameleon is considered the world's largest chameleon by weight and one of the largest by length. They are the smallest chameleon species and the smallest reptile.
